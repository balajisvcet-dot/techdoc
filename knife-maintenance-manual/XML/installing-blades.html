Installing Blades

Note:

Install lower blade first. The lower blade is the fixed blade and is bolted in place with the blade's back against the holder. The upper blade has slotted mounting holes and is adjustable. The two end bolts are 0.38 in NC socket head cap screws while the remaining bolts are 0.31 x 0.50 in hex head screws. Always use new bolts as supplied in the blade kits when installing new blades.

  1. Lay new blade on holder. Position blade laterally until holes in center of blade are directly centered over the threaded holes in holder.
  2. Install blade bolts starting in center and working alternately out toward the ends of blade. Bolts will have to be torqued until snug to allow insertion of all bolts along the length of the blade.
  3. Torque all bolts to 12 Nm (110 in-lb), again starting at center bolt and working alternately out toward ends of blade.
  4. Force the blade tightly against blade holder. Place a piece of hardwood, UHMWPE plastic or tool # 9356417 against blade and strike with a hammer (1.4 to 1.8 kg [3 to 4 lb]) to force blade against back of holder. Repeat at 50 to 100 mm (2 to 4 in) intervals while working from right to left (looking upstream). Blade must be no greater than 0.07 mm (0.003 in) from holder. Check gap with a feeler gauge.
  5. Torque blade bolts to 25 Nm (220 in-lb).
  6. Torque socket head bolt at each end of blade to 34 Nm (300 in-lb).
  7. Repeat Steps 1 through 6 for the upper blade.
  8. Proceed to Setting Cylinder Gear Preload procedure in Maintenance section.
