Fluid and Filter Replacement Procedure

  1. Locate Valve (A) and Plug (B) on the back side of the radiator.


  2. Nipple (5080430) and a quick disconnect (3260030) to be installed if they are not already there.


  3. Connect pump kit to radiator.
  4. Pump kit with electric drill (not supplied) shown below.


  5. Use electric drill as pump requires faster speed. Do not use cordless drill.
  6. Place open end of pump kit hose in a bucket or container that can hold approximately 12-15 gallons [45-56 liters] of fluid.
  7. Open Valve (A) and spin electric drill forward to drain.
  8. Remove any lines that may have not drained under gravity and allow them to drain completely.
    Note:

    Cover Electric components in the enclosure as needed to avoid any fluid spill on to them

    Note:

    Used fluid should never be put back into knife.

  9. Remove filter (C).


  10. After draining is complete install new filter, part number 80417650. Install any disconnected lines and manifold plugs. Use pipe thread sealant, part number 3960124 on the threads. Do not use Teflon tape.
  11. New fluid part number is 9425230. It will require between 12 and 15 gallons per side.
  12. Filling new fluid can be done in two methods.
    1. Filling bottoms up: Place the open end of pump kit in the new fluid bucket, spin the electric drill reverse, fill until sight gage is half full, lock the valve (A) once done. Disconnect Pump kit from Radiator. This method is recommended for large fill.
    2. Filling top down: Pour liquid from top by opening the lid (D) from top of the knife. Close the lid once done. This method is recommended for small fill.


    Warning:

    If heat transfer fluids other than Paratherm NF are used in or added to the knife, no warranties will be recognized for motors, drives, cooling systems, or other related items.

  13. After knife is back in operation for several hours recheck fluid level. If needed fill from top as this needs small amount of fluid.
    Note:

    If fluid has become contaminated by water or another foreign substance the above steps may have to be repeated several times to flush the system. Report any findings back to BW Papersystems field service as soon as possible.
