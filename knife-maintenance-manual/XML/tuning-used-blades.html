Tuning Used Blades

Tune entire blade if cut quality becomes unacceptable on a large portion of the blade or as soon as possible after a spot tune.

DANGER

Blades are extremely sharp. Keep hands and fingers away from blades.

CAUTION:

Never rotate cylinders forward through a cut with blade lapping compound on the blades.

Note:

Use cut-resistant gloves to protect hands from sharp blades.

Note:

BW Papersystems recommends tuning used blades immediately after the machine is taken out of production while it is still at operating temperature.

Note:

Reset gear preload if a sudden loss of cut quality over a wide area of blades occurs. To check gear preload before tuning used blades, see Checking Cylinder Gear Preload procedure in Maintenance section.

  1. Review and perform Gaining Access to Knife Cylinders procedure in Maintenance section.
  2. Apply electrical tape to the seam where the blade meets the blade holder (upper and lower blades).
  3. To restore blade tips, apply fine grit (320) lapping compound to upper blade when using super sharp tip blades.
  4. Lap blades.
    1. Review and perform Turning Power On procedure in Operation section including the E-Stop reset and Knife Safety Circuit OK reset functions.
      Note:

      Blades will not lap with Automatic on.

    2. Verify all safety gates around the knife are closed and that the Bus power LED is on.
    3. On the HMI, select 'Knife’ from the Real-time display.
    4. Then select ‘Initiate Blade Lapping’.
    5. A window requesting a password will appear. Enter the current Maintenance password for your machine and press ‘Enter’. The blades start lapping immediately after pressing ‘Enter’.
    6. The lapping process will automatically stop after 500 revolutions of the knife cylinder, which takes approximately 5 minutes. To stop the blades from lapping earlier than the 500 revolutions, deselect the 'Lapping in progress' button in the Knife menu screen.
    7. Repeat step 1 before proceeding.
    8. Clean the used lapping compound from the upper and lower blade using paperboard triangles, and wipe clean with a rag.
  5. Repeat Steps 3 and 4 two more times.
  6. Using a graduated microscope, inspect upper and lower blade lapping areas for sharp tips (Figure 9).
  7. Inspect blade tips.
    1. If a sharp tip is obtained, proceed to Step 8.
    2. If a sharp tip is not obtained: Blades will need to be brought closer together for the lapping compound to work.
      1. In areas where tip is not sharp, advance blade by one twelfth of a turn on adjusting screw. Advance to achieve cut with dry newspaper.
      2. Repeat steps from Step 3 above.
  8. Remove tape and excess lapping compound.
  9. Starting from right and working left looking upstream, final tune blades to cut wet newspaper across the entire length of the blade. Advance upper blade by turning the adjusting screws a maximum of one twelfth of a turn.
    Note:

    If final tune is being done within 2 hours after machine was taken out of production, tune blades to cut every fiber of the wet newspaper.

    Note:

    If final tune is being made after machine has cooled down (approximately 2 hours), DO NOT TUNE BLADES TO CUT EVERY FIBER OF THE WET NEWSPAPER. Blades will become too tight when machine reaches operating temperature. It is always better to retune than to tune too tightly and destroy blades.

  10. Manually rotate blades forward through cut.
    Note:

    Torque on manual rotation shaft needed to pass blades through a cut may be slightly higher than torque to rotate cylinders when not in cutting position. This torque difference should not be more than 7 Nm (60 in-lb) for sharp tip blades or 3 Nm (24 in-lb) for super sharp tip blades. If torque increase is greater than maximum values or blades will not pass through cut (NEVER FORCE THE BLADES THROUGH THE CUT), blades are adjusted too tightly together. See Loosening Tight Blades procedure in Maintenance section.

  11. Tighten adjusting screw locknuts.
  12. Apply an oil film to both blades.
  13. Remove tools and return machine to operational status.
