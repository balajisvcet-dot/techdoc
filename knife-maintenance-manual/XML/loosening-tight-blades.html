Loosening Tight Blades

During tuning or replacement, if either blade refuses to pass or the differential torque needed to rotate blades through cut exceeds 7 Nm (60 in-lb) for sharp tip blades or 3 Nm (24 in-lb) for super sharp tip blades, loosen blades.

DANGER

Blades are extremely sharp. Keep hands and fingers away from blades.

Note:

All adjustment is to be done on upper blade only. do not adjust lower blade.

  1. Check blades for tight areas. Rotate blades out of cutting position. Smooth over any rough spots with honing stone.
  2. Loosen adjusting screws near tight area allowing slight blade retraction.
  3. Recheck blade pass. If blades pass, continue with tuning procedure.
  4. If the blades do not pass, locate and mark high-resistance area.
  5. Back off five adjusting screws to left and right of tight area.
  6. Loosen and retorque the five blade bolts to left and right of tight area to 12 Nm (110 in-lb) one at a time.
  7. Place clean piece of wood or UHMWPE plastic, or tool # 9356417 on tight area of blade. With a 1.4 to 1.8 kg (3 to 4 lb) hammer, force blade back against the holder.
  8. Recheck blade pass. If blades do not pass, repeat Steps 4 through 7.
  9. Retorque blade bolts one at a time to 25 Nm (220 in-lb) of torque for tuning purposes.
  10. Resume tuning procedure.
