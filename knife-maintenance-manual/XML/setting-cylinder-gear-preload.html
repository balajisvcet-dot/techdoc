Setting Cylinder Gear Preload

Preloading cylinder gears and setting initial position may be required when installing new blades.

DANGER

Blades are extremely sharp. Keep hands and fingers away from blades.

Note:

Use cut-resistant gloves to protect hands from sharp blades.

  1. Review and perform Gaining Access to Knife Cylinders in Maintenance section.
  2. Loosen all 16 upper cylinder hub locking nuts.


  3. Check if blades will pass forward (board flow direction) through the cut. If not, back off screw A and advance D in one quarter turn increments until blades pass.
  4. In this process Advancing D and backing off A will open the blades. Advancing A and backing off D will close or make the blades tighter.
  5. Make test cuts.
    1. Using dry newspaper, make test cuts along blade from right to left, looking upstream.
      CAUTION:

      Never force blades through a cut or damage to the blades may occur.

    2. If dry newspaper is cut anywhere along blade, proceed to Step 6. If not, back off screw D by one eighth of a turn and advance screw A by an eighth of a turn increment and repeat Step 3. Continue this procedure until the dry newspaper is cut somewhere along blade.
  6. Prepare hub lock stud by removing both nuts, lock washers, and brackets from the two hub lock studs located adjacent to screw D. Thread the two removed nuts onto the hub lock stud located adjacent and to the right of screw D. Jam the two nuts together at least 6 mm (0.25 in) above the cylinder, ensuring the top nut has a face parallel to the cylinder.
  7. Mount the dial indicator on the cylinder, so the dial indicator meets the flat surface of upper nut on prepared stud. The dial indicator shaft should be perpendicular to the prepared stud.
    Note:

    Dial indicator must be mounted on cylinder, not on machine frame, gear case.

  8. Ensure screw B & C is backed out far enough so it does not contact push stud when screw D is advanced.
  9. Remove gear backlash.
    1. Alternately advance and release screw D, advancing 0.1 mm (0.005 in) further each time until spring back is obtained.
    2. At first sign of spring back, continue backing off screw D until spring back stops. This is gear contact point from which gear preload is put in. (Refer to table 4 for proper cylinder gear preload.)

      CYLINDER GEAR PRELOAD CHART

      		  

      KNIFE WIDTH

      PRELOAD

      1650 mm (65 inch)

      0.20 mm (0.008 inch)

      1850 mm (73 inch)

      0.25 mm (0.010 inch)

      2500mm (98 inch)

      0.33 mm (0.013 inch)
  10. Set gear preload. Set the dial indicator to zero, and advance screw D until dial indicator shows correct movement. (Refer to table 4) The gear preload is set.
    Note:

    Do not change settings after gear backlash has been removed and gear preload has been set. To retain gear preload, advance screw A and back off screw D in equal amounts. This allows initial position of cylinders to be set without changing gear preload.

    CAUTION:

    Never force blades through a cut or damage to the blades may occur.

  11. Remove dial indicator. Pass blades forward through cut to check for blade binding. If blades pass, go to Step 14.
  12. If blades bind during cut, open the blades. Back off screw A by an eighth turn and advance screw D by an equal eighth turn. Pass blades through cut. Repeat Step 12 until blades pass.
  13. Retorque gear preload studs as follows:
    1. Torque all 16 hub lock nuts to 45 Nm (400 in-lb).
    2. Torque all 16 hub lock studs to their final torque of 90 Nm (800 in-lb).
    3. Hand tighten screw B against push stud.
    4. Torque screw B to 6 Nm (50 in-lb).
    5. Torque screw A to 11 Nm (100 in-lb).
    6. Torque screw B to 11 Nm (100 in-lb).
    7. Final torque screw A to 17 Nm (150 in-lb).
    8. Final torque screw B to 17 Nm (150 in-lb).
    9. Hand tighten screw C against push stud.
    10. Torque screw C to 6 Nm (50 in-lb).
    11. Torque screw D to 11 Nm (100 in-lb).
    12. Torque screw C to 11 Nm (100 in-lb).
    13. Final torque screw D to 17 Nm (150 in-lb).
    14. Final torque screw C to 17 Nm (150 in-lb).
  14. Proceed to Tuning New Blades procedure in Maintenance section.
