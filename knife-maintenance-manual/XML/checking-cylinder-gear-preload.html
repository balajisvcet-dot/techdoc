Checking Cylinder Gear Preload

If cut quality deteriorates instantly across the full length of blade at some point during a normal tune interval, gear preload may have been lost.

  1. Review and perform Gaining Access to Knife Cylinders procedure in Maintenance sections.
  2. Loosen eight hub locking studs on right end of upper cylinder.
  3. Prepare hub lock stud by removing both nuts, lock washers, and brackets. from the two hub lock studs located adjacent to screw D (Figure 10). Thread the two removed nuts onto the hub lock stud located adjacent and to the right of screw D. Jam the two nuts together at least 6 mm (0.25 in) above the cylinder, making sure the top nut has a face parallel to the cylinder.
  4. Mount dial indicator on cylinder, so the indicator meets the flat surface of upper nut on the prepared stud. The dial indicator shaft should be perpendicular to the prepared stud.
    Note:

    Dial indicator must be mounted on cylinder, not on machine frame, gear case, or exit roll assembly.

  5. Back off screw D until loose, then a half turn additional. Note indicator movement.
  6. Advance screw C until 0.4 mm (0.015 in) of additional indicator movement is observed.
  7. Back off screw C one full turn.
  8. Advance screw D in 0.1 mm (0.005 in) increments, backing off each time and watching for reverse dial indicator movement. At first sign of reverse movement, continue backing off screw D until this movement stops. This is gear contact point. Indicator should show 0.33 mm (0.013 in) movement from original position noted in Step 5.
  9. Set gear preload.
    1. Position dial indicator to zero.
    2. Advance screw D for 0.33 mm (0.013 in) of movement. (The torque to obtain 0.33 mm [0.013 in] of movement should not exceed 8 Nm[75 in-lb].)
  10. Retorque loosened bolts as follows:
    1. Torque all eight hub lock bolts to 45 N-m (400 in-lb).
    2. Torque all eight hub lock bolts to 90 N-m (800 in-lb).
    3. Hand tighten screw C against push stud.
    4. Set torque wrench to 6 N-m (50 in-lb), and confirm that screw D meets or exceeds 6 N-m (50 in-lb).
    5. Torque screw C to 6 N-m (50 in-lb).
    6. Torque screw D to 11 N-m (100 in-lb).
    7. Torque screw C to 11 N-m (100 in-lb).
    8. Torque screw D to 17 N-m (150 in-lb).
    9. Torque screw C to 17 N-m (150 in-lb).
