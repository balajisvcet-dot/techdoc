Spot Tuning Used Blades

Only spot tune blades if cut quality becomes unacceptable on a portion of the blades, or if a complete blade tuning procedure cannot be done immediately. Perform a complete tune as soon as possible after a spot tune.

DANGER

Blades are extremely sharp. Keep hands and fingers away from blades.

CAUTION:

Never rotate cylinders forward through a cut with blade lapping compound on blades.

Note:

Use cut-resistant gloves to protect hands from sharp blades.

  1. Review and perform Gaining Access to Knife Cylinders in Maintenance section.
  2. Before entering machine, determine area of poor cut quality.
  3. Upon entering machine, verify poor cut quality areas by using wet newspaper and testing cut across the entire width of the blade. Mark areas of concern on cylinder with a pen.
  4. Apply electrical tape to the seam where the blade meets the blade holder. (upper and lower blades).
  5. Apply lapping compound to marked areas of upper blade and 254 mm (10 in) on each side of marked area. Use fine grit (320) on super sharp tip blades. Apply an oil film to the rest of the blade area (upper and lower).
  6. Lap blades.
    1. Review and perform Turning Power On procedure in Operation section including the E-Stop reset and Knife Safety Circuit OK reset functions.
      Note:

      Blades will not lap with Automatic on.

    2. Verify all safety gates around the knife are closed and that Bus power LED is on.
    3. On the HMI, select 'Knife’ from the Real-time display.
    4. Then select ‘Initiate Blade Lapping’.
    5. A window requesting a password will appear. Enter the current Maintenance password for your machine and press ‘Enter’. The blades start lapping immediately after pressing ‘Enter’.
    6. The lapping process will automatically stop after 500 revolutions of the knife cylinder, which takes approximately 5 minutes. To stop the blades from lapping earlier than the 500 revolutions, deselect the 'Lapping in progress' button in the Knife menu screen.
    7. Repeat step 1 before proceeding.
    8. Clean the used lapping compound from the upper and lower blades using paperboard triangles, and wipe clean with a rag.
  7. Repeat Steps 5 through 6 two more times.
  8. Using a graduated microscope, inspect upper and lower blade lapping areas for a sharp tip (Figure 9 below).
  9. Inspect blade tips.
    1. If a sharp tip is obtained, proceed to Step 10.
    2. If a sharp tip is not obtained: Blades will need to be brought closer together for the lapping compound to work.
      1. In areas where tip is not sharp, advance blade by one twelfth of a turn on adjusting screw. Advance to achieve cut with dry newspaper.
      2. Repeat steps from step 5 above.

  10. Remove tape and excess lapping compound.
  11. Starting from right and working left looking upstream, final tune blades to cut wet newspaper across the entire length of the blade. Advance upper blade by turning the adjusting screws a maximum of one twelfth of a turn.
    Note:

    If final tune is being done within 2 hours after machine was taken out of production, tune blades to cut every fiber of the wet newspaper.

    Note:

    If final tune is being made after machine has cooled down (approximately 2 hours), DO NOT TUNE BLADES TO CUT EVERY FIBER OF THE WET NEWSPAPER. Blades will become too tight when machine reaches operating temperature. It is always better to retune than to tune too tightly and destroy blades.

  12. Manually rotate blades forward through cut.
    Note:

    Torque on manual rotation shaft needed to pass blades through a cut may be slightly higher than torque needed to rotate cylinders when not in cutting position. This torque difference should not be more than 7 Nm (60 in-lb) for sharp tip blades or 3 Nm (24 in-lb) for super sharp blades. If torque increase is greater than maximum values or blades will not pass through cut (NEVER FORCE BLADES THROUGH CUT), blades are adjusted too tightly together. See Loosening Tight Blades procedure in Maintenance section.

    Warning:
  13. Remove tools and return machine to operational status.
    DANGER

    Do not spot tune more than twice without a complete retune.

  14. Schedule downtime to completely re-tune blades.
