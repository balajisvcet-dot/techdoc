Cylinder Motor Replacement

  1. Perform Safety Lockout procedure in Operation section.
  2. If motor #1 is not being replaced, skip Steps 3, 4, and 5.
  3. Loosen resolver coupling screws nearest the cylinder motor.
  4. Remove resolver mounting screws and carefully pull resolver and coupling off the mounting stand.
    Note:

    The coupling is a flexible type and can be damaged by excessive force.

  5. Remove mounting stand, stud, and nut from the cylinder motor.
  6. Install 0.375 in NC x 1.50 in bolt (Item 1, Figure 14) and 0.375 in jam nut (2) onto rear end of rotor shaft on the cylinder motor (4).
    Note:

    The cylinder motor has a timing mark scribed on the rotor shaft and motor case as shown in figure 17.

    Figure 1. Cylinder Motor (with Right Hand Pinion Gear)

  7. Rotate rotor shaft on the cylinder motor with 0.375 in NC x 1.50 in bolt (1) until the timing mark on the rotor shaft is aligned with the scribe mark on the outside casing of the cylinder motor. Remove bolt and jam nut (2).
    Note:

    During rotation of the rotor shaft, the cylinders will also rotate and all other motors will be moved to their timed positions.

  8. Drain the cooling system fluid (See “Cooling System Fluid and Filter Replacement” for the steps on how to drain the fluid). Dispose of old coolant.
  9. Remove coolant hoses from faulty cylinder motor by loosening the hose barb in the swivel fitting and then backing off the nut on the swivel fitting.
    Note:

    The hose barb will stay attached to the hose and can be reused on the new cylinder motor.

  10. Disconnect armature wires from drive and remove ground wire from ground bar.
  11. Loosen nut connecting flexible conduit to divider wall.
  12. Remove cylinder motor by removing bolts with socket wrench (BW Papersystems part number 9416511) included in the BW Papersystems tool kit.
    CAUTION:

    The cylinder motor weighs 113 kg (250 lb). To prevent potential injury by lifting cylinder motor by hand, use a lifting jig (BW Papersystems part number 9446283) and fork truck.

    Note:

    Be sure to note the position of any shims on the motor mounting plate. The shims must remain in the same position during reassembly.

    CAUTION:

    During cylinder motor removal, damage to Knife components could occur from residual oil spilled from the motor. Use extreme care when removing cylinder motor to prevent spilling oil.

  13. Remove flexible conduit and fittings after cylinder motor removal. Mount flexible conduit and fittings onto new cylinder motor.
  14. Verify motor pinion gear on the new cylinder motor matches the one on the old motor.

  15. Before sliding the new cylinder motor into position, rotate timing mark on the rotor shaft approximately 45° as shown in Figure 16. When new cylinder motor is slid into position, timing marks will be aligned.
  16. Since pinion gears have an odd number of geared teeth, the cylinder motor may be rotated 180° out of its timed position. If timing mark of the rotor shaft is not aligned within 4° of the motor scribe mark, as shown in Figure 17, rotate pinion 180° and reposition cylinder motor.
    Note:

    The timing mark on rotor shaft must be within 4° of the motor scribe mark for the cylinder motor to run efficiently.

    Figure 3. Rotation Motor Shaft

  17. Before installing motor mounting bolts, ensure shims are in positions noted in Step 12.
    Note:

    Shim thickness is stamped on Knife frame side plates in thousandths of an inch.

  18. Install motor mounting bolts. Tighten bolts to 39 N-m (29 ft-lb).
  19. Check motor backlash measurement at four different positions on the Knife cylinder using a dial indicator (Figure 18). Ensure locations are 90° apart on the cylinder gear. To reach each position, rotate the rotor shaft of the cylinder motor 240° or 0.666 turns. Perform this step four times and write down the motor backlash measurement and cylinder position.

    1

    Dial Indicator
  20. Return the Knife cylinder to the closest point of the backlash measurement.Set the backlash with the jackbolts mounted at the side of the frame to 0.203 mm (0.008 in) at a point 178 mm (7 in) from the motor shaft.
  21. Torque motor mounting bolts to 142 N-m (105 ft-lb). Recheck motor backlash measurement according to Step 19.
  22. Connect the armature leads to the drive and the ground wire to the ground bar.
    Note:

    Do not cut armature leads. The leads must remain at their original lengths. Excess wire can be stored in wireway.

  23. Reattach coolant hoses onto the motor, ensuring a good seal between the motor and all liquid fittings.
  24. Add new coolant (BW Papersystems part number 9425230) and replace cooling system filter.(BW Papersystems part number 80417650). Follow cooling system fluid and filter replacement procedure in maintenance section.
    Note:

    The coolant level should be at least halfway on the sight gauge when the cooling system is cold. Additional coolant may need to be added after the system has run and the air has been purged (see General Checks Schedule in Maintenance section) through the clear hose located between the return manifold and the reservoir.

    Note:

    If stud from old cylinder motor is worn, replace stud.

  25. If motor #1 is not being replaced, Cylinder Motor Replacement procedure is complete and skip Steps 26, 27, 28, and 29.
  26. Mount stud (Figure 19) and nut into new cylinder motor. Ensure distance from edge of nut to end of stud is 19 mm(0.75 in). Tighten nut and stud firmly.
    Note:

    Apply Loctite 242 on stud threads.

  27. Mount dial indicator (Item 2, Figure 20) to back of cylinder motor. While rotating motor shaft, use a hammer to straighten out stud. Maximum run out should not exceed ± 0.05 mm (0.002 in).
  28. Reinstall mounting stand onto cylinder motor.
  29. Reinstall cylinder motor resolver according to Cylinder Motor Resolver Replacement procedure in Maintenance section.
