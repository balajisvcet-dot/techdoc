Tuning New Blades

DANGER

Blades are extremely sharp. Keep hands and fingers away from blades.

CAUTION:

Never rotate cylinders forward through a cut with blade lapping compound on the blades.

Note:

Use cut-resistant gloves to protect hands from INFORMATION sharp blades.

Use care when tuning new, cold blades with wet 440 g/sq m (90 lb) paper or wet newspaper. Do not try to cut every fiber clean or your blades will be too tight. It is better to tune loose and retune than to tune tightly and destroy blades.

Note:

As this is an initial tune, you may need to retune the blades within2 weeks.

Before proceeding, see Setting Cylinder Gear Preload procedure in Maintenance section. Gear preload and initial position must be set.

  1. Review and perform Gaining Access to Knife Cylinders procedure in Maintenance section.
  2. Confirm that all upper blade bolts are torqued to 25 Nm (220 in-lb) and all lower blade bolts to 25 Nm (220 in-lb).
  3. Apply electrical tape to the seam between the blade and blade holder.
  4. Rough tune blades to cut dry newspaper across entire width of blade; most, but not all, fibers should be cut. Do not advance blade by turning adjusting screw more than one twelfth of a turn.
  5. Manually rotate blades forward through cut. At no time should the increase in torque on the blades going through the cut dry exceed 7 Nm (60 in-lb) for sharp tip blades or 3 Nm (24 in-lb) for super sharp tip blades. If torque required to rotate blades through cut has increased or blades will not pass through the cut (NEVER FORCE THE BLADES THROUGH CUT), you have adjusted blades too tightly together. See Loosening Tight Blades procedure in Maintenance section.
  6. Apply lapping compound to upper blade only. Use fine grit lapping compound (320) on super sharp tip blades.
  7. Lap blades.
    1. Review and perform Turning Power On procedure in Operation section including the E-Stop reset and Knife Safety Circuit OK reset functions.
      Note:

      Blades will not lap with Automatic on.

    2. Verify all safety gates around the knife are closed and the bus power LED is on.
    3. On the HMI, select 'Knife’ from the Real-time display.
    4. Then select ‘Initiate Blade Lapping’.
    5. A window requesting a password will appear. Enter the current Maintenance password for your machine and press ‘Enter’. The blades start lapping immediately after pressing ‘Enter’.
    6. The lapping process will automatically stop after 500 revolutions of the knife cylinder, which takes approximately 5 minutes. To stop the blades from lapping earlier than the 500 revolutions, deselect the 'Lapping in progress' button in the Knife menu screen.To stop the blades from lapping, deselect the 'Lap Blades' button on the Knife control screen.
    7. Repeat step 1 before proceeding.
    8. Clean the used lapping compound from the upper and lower blades using paperboard triangles, and wipe clean with a rag.
  8. Repeat Step 6 and 7 two more times.
  9. Using graduated microscope, inspect upper and lower blade lapping areas for a minimum 0.254mm (0.010 in) surface wear measured down from lower blade tip (Figure 9).
    1. If you see the minimum surface wear, continue with Step 10.
    2. If minimum surface wear is not obtained: Blades will need to be brought closer together for the lapping compound to work.
      1. In areas where tip does not show surface wear advance blade by one twelfth of a turn on adjusting screw. Advance to achieve cut with dry newspaper.
      2. Repeat steps from Step 6.
  10. Remove tape and excess lapping compound.
  11. Final tune blades to cut wet 440 g/sq m (90-lb) paper or wet newspaper (not every fiber) across entire width of blade. Do not advance blade by turning adjusting screw more than one twelfth of a turn at a time.
  12. Manually rotate blades forward through cut.
    Note:

    Torque on manual rotation shaft needed to pass blades through a cut may be slightly higher than torque to rotate cylinders when not in cutting position. This torque difference should not be more than 7Nm (60in-lb) for sharp tip blades or 3Nm (24 in-lb) for super sharp tip blades. If torque increase is greater than maximum values or blades will not pass through cut (NEVER FORCE THE BLADES THROUGH THE CUT), blades are adjusted too tightly together. See Loosening Tight Blades procedure in Maintenance section.

  13. Tighten the adjusting screw’s locknuts.
  14. Apply an oil film to both blades.
  15. Remove tools and return machine to operational status.
